Blog Post 3. Team Work and Working with Clients

Chapter 19

I would like to talk about a negative experience I have when working on a team project. It is impossible to assign tasks evenly to every single one of the team and sometimes this discrepancy will result in more conflicts between group members. There’s a time when I and a couple of friends gather together and work for a Software Engineering class. We split the work into several parts: write ups , UI/UX, front-end, back-end and testing. Each one will be responsible for one part. We separate those work by functions not by work load. It turns out the one who works on the back-end need to spend much more time since he also needs to work on the implementation of database. Then we have a argument since he feels it is unfair for him to work that much. As the team leader, I spent lots of time to help coordinate but both sides were not happy with the collaboration. I think Anderson’s guideline that we need to establish shared responsibility for team progress and cohesion gonna be very useful here. I assign the work based on each one’s expertise and if you take a part you need to be responsible for that part. And sometimes it is not fair to judge based on actual workload. If I can I will make each one’s progress more transparent so that everyone can get know their contribution corresponds to the team’s general progress and I believe it will make the collaboration part more smooth.

Chapter 21

I think the guideline determine what your client wants and why gonna be a crucial part in our communication. As what have already mentioned in the expected questions in the interview, we will try to figure out details about the client’s requirements before we actually work on it. The time cost for the consultation will be much less than the time cost for those later modifications. Also, if we can determine what your client wants and why in advance. We can come up with a complete plan for the development which will convince the client to choose our service and help ourselves to make a better allocation of resources.
