Blog Posting 4. Proposals and Statements of Work (SOW)

Part 1: Answer the following questions

  • Which of the three situations on pp. 492-93 does your team face?
    • We face the When Your Readers Define the Problem for You situation for our project.
  • Therefore, what tip(s) can you glean from those pages that you’ll apply to your Statement of Work?
    • We need to find a way to describe the problme in our own words corresponds with and build on the readers’ description.
  • What problem or lack is your system meant to solve? (If there are more than one, list them in the most logical order.)
    • Optimize the algorithms used in matching process of mentor and mentee.
    • Combine the previous visual design and app prototype together to make a working demo.
  • Who is affected?
    • Mentor (Upper Class student)
    • Mentee (Incoming new freshman)
    • Administrator
  • Are there similar systems in existence, and if so, why can’t they be used?
    • Yes, there’s currently alternative from enterprise software companies. They are too expensive and does not have appealing UI.
  • What is the motivation for the system to be developed?
    • Help to bring incoming freshman and fellow students together.

Part 2: Compare different versions of problem statement

  • Sample 1: Safe House
    • The old problem statement keeps talking about current system and current map system while the final version split the problem to different parts(solutions) of the current system and talks about the limitness of them seperately.
    • The final version shows a more exhaustive list of current solutions and have more complete description on what they can do and can’t do, which is more convincing when we trying to develope something new to replace the old ones.
  • Sample 2: HealthCare
    • Compared with the original version, the final problem statement also includes problem from the perspective of doctor and has a more direct connection between use case and disadvantages.
    • The final version improves a lot since it talks about the disadvantages of the current system for both patients and doctors instead of only patients. It also helps people realize such disadvantages in real use case more directly.

Part 3: Rank problem statements

  • Safe House
  • HealthCare
  • Website Redesign

I rank the safe house in the first place since list all the alternatives and provide enough information about all of them. I can easily get what current system can do and can not do. It is much better than simply listing those disadvantages. HealthCare is on the second place, it talks about weakness of current system from two different target group of users, but it will be better if the writer can directly separate the problem statement into two parts. Website redesign is the last one. The writer talks a lot about what needs to be improved but these just seem to be too much. It will be better to figure out a way to categorize and organize them.
