Blog Posting 5. Drafting Prose and Designing Documents

Chapter 8

After reading chapter 8, I found the guideline that present your generalizations before your details very useful. It is similar to the the idea of topic sentence that we talked before. It is good to present the general ideas in advance. For example, if I want to convince people using Node.js in a blog post. It will be better for me to list the key features in each paragraph before going to details. If I put asynchronous and multithreading in the beginning of paragraphs. Readers will definitely feel interest and go ahead to explore what is in the paragraph. If I put too much unnecessary stuff before going to the actual part, readers may already feel bored before going actual useful information.

Another guideline I feel very useful is consider your reader’s cultural background when organizing. Context is always a very important part i communication. People from different background will have different thinking mode. If you can cater into that, it will be much easier to convince them. For instance, Germans may like accurate statistics, Chinese may like compliments while jokes will be okay for people from United States. It’s not about stereotype but about techniques in communication. If you understand others as good as yourself, it will be much easier to communicate with them.

Chapter 16

I’m really interested in the role of design in highlighting and presenting the subject matter. It reminds me of my daily experience of cellphone usage and web browsing. I tend to read more with app with good UI/UX designs. That is also the reason why big companies like Google and Microsoft change their visual identities. Actually, resume will be such a good example for this topic. Previously, I have all my information stacked on my resume. It is true that I have lots of information included in the resume. But it’s so hard to find bullet points. Everything seems to be on the same level and it is even hard to identify between different sections. Then I start using LaTex for my resume, bold and underline all the section titles. After that, I use bullet points to list technology used and my role for each project. It turns out to be a good change since now it is much easier for recruiters to get useful information from a single sheet of paper.
What surprised me here is that physical features is also an element of communication. We usually tend to focus more on the content. I have never thought of physical features here. Different texture or shape will give you different feelings when expressing the same idea.
