Blog Posting 7. Feasibility Reports

Part 1: Please comment on Chapter 6. What could be applied to CS research? How?

With correspondence to the theme of this tech communication class. This chapter on research also has an emphasis on reader-centered approaches. And I found the part of How to gather information and ideas your readers want and need most useful. The main goal for conducting research is to gather information and this part is about detailed approaches we take to gather information.

The guideline use secondary sources your readers will find credible and unbiased is very useful. We should be able to differ from rumors and fact. When I was in the position of a reader of a research report, if the writer directly uses statistics or arguments from some website, I will highly doubt the correctness behind it. And if the writer uses some information he or she gets from a published academic report. I will definitely find it more persuasive. Such a guideline actually corresponds to the requirement of using citations in other communication class. A convincing endorsement will make your argument more powerful.

Another guideline use primary research methods in a credible and unbiased way illustrate the same point as above. And it is a complementation of the guideline that identify the full range of sources and methods that may provide helpful information.

Part 3: Examine the FR examples: Example 1, Example 2. Compare the Evaluation sections in these two documents. Think about better ways to have communicated the results to the audience and include these ideas/suggestions in your comments.

Sample 1 and Sample 2 have different order of aligning evaluation chart and descriptions. Sample 1 put the descriptions at first and then display the chart while Sample 2 does it in the reverse way. I think the layout of Sample 2 is better since it provides a general idea (conclusion) first, an interesting or a huge contrast between alternatives will attract the reader to dig more.

Another difference between Sample 1 and Sample 2 is that in Sample, it uses relative measurements such as “more” or “less” which is kind of very vague here. However, in Sample 2, it uses actual statistics for comparison. It’s more convincing than simply saying “good” or “bad”.
