Blog Posting 8. Beginning and Ending a Document

Part 1: Which guidelines from Chapter 11 did the authors follow in the Introduction of Sample 1? How? Which guidelines from Chapter 11 did the authors follow in the Introduction of Sample 2? How? What would be some of the ways to improve these introductions?

  • Sample 1
    • State your main point
    • Provide a forecasting statement
    • Include a summary

The writer starts the introduction part by giving a brief context description. After a short preparation, the writer follows the State your main point guideline by describing the problem they’re trying to solve. At the same time, the writer starts forecasting what the solution might be by referring to the things expected in the final solution. Then, a very short conclusion is given at the very end. Overall, the introduction section provides a way for readers to get to know the material that gonna be covered in the report before actually reading it.

  • Sample 2
    • State the main point
    • Include a summary

Considering the introduction section, sample 2 follows a similar approach that sample 1 does. The writer first state the main point after a brief background description. Then the writer talks about potential alternatives. After that, he or she uses a conclusion to end the introduction. After reading the whole introduction part, I feel like it is a little bit too technical. It will be better if the writer can include more background information.

Part 2: Which guidelines from Chapter 12 did the authors follow in the Conclusions/Recommendations of Sample 1? How? Which guidelines from Chapter 11 did the authors follow in the Conclusions/Recommendations of Sample 2? How? What would be some of the ways to improve these sections?

  • Sample 1
    • Repeat your main point
    • Summarize your key points

Sample 1 does a summary on all the discussions before in the report for the conclusion part. However, it does not directly and explicitly repeat all the points mentioned above. On the contrast, the writer repeat all the small comparison and evaluation result again to convince the readers. It will be better if the writer can use bullet points to make a correspondence with the criteria before.

  • Sample 2
    • Summarize your key points

Similar to sample 1, sample 1 does a summary on all the discussions before int the report for the conclusion part. It does not repeat all the main points, instead, it only focused on several main comparison. After those comparison, the writer gives a suggestion on which alternative to choose. And then he or she elaborates more on the alternative chosen. I feel that it has the same problem as sample 1. It will be better if the writer can use bullet points to make a correspondence with the criteria before.
