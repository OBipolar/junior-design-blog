Presentations Posting

Presentation has become a very important approach to deliver ideas in both school and work. But people always commit different kinds of mistakes during the process. As we discussed in during class: sometimes people can not control their voice and prose well, sometimes they have terrible slides layout.

When I give my presentations, I think I have some specific strength. One thing that I am proud of is that I always have a good interaction with my audience. Of course I will prepare before the presentation. But all the preparations are based on my own expectation on how my audience will perceive the information that I was trying to deliver. During the actual process of the presentation, I will try to observe how audience respond to the material and make some small adjustment and even make some improvisation if necessary. This will help the presentation go more smoothly.

Something I did not perform that well must be the content of the presentation. I always focused more on the general idea instead of details since I feel like idas, relationship etc on the general level can reveal more about the nature of the problem. However, without details, it is not convincing enough. By including a couple of examples during the presentation, I think this problem can then be solved.

Blog Post 10. Presentations

Part 1: Write a response to Anderson’s Ch. 20.

After reading chapter 20, the guideline that I find most useful is ”Choose your visual medium by considering your audience, topic, and purpose”. I think it is a very interesting point. We usually emphasize too much on the content of presentation. It is true that the idea we are trying to deliver is very very important. But the medium and methodology that we take to approach the actual presentation also matters.

The choice of the visual medium will heavily influence how your audience perceive the ideas. Slides can help to organize the material with bullet points but may be lack of details. A video can be perfect when you trying to show how a app works but will take much longer time to prepare. A poster will be super fast to prepare and can help at the situation without computers. In general, a wise decision on which visual medium to apply will make your presentation much more effective.

My personal methodology is to put a single page PDF with a couple of bullet points and probably hierarchy map. One thing I believe is that presentation is not about reading materials that you have prepared. It should be more like a show where you display your ideas. I will elaborate each point and talk about the relationship between all those points. With both details and general view, my audience can always get the idea that I am trying to deliver.

The recent presentation on the Surface Book by Microsoft gives me a very good impression. It is Microsoft’s annually press release of new products. The first good thing about it is the content. WOVEN Posting

Possible Communication Genres:

  • Email: W + E + V
    • Email contains words(W) and sometimes includes electronic attachments(E). It may also have pictures or videos embeded(V).
  • Face to Face Communication: O + N
    • When speak(O) to people, we also use signs or body language(N) to help the communication.
  • Presentation: W + O + V + E + N
    • When doing a presentation, we first need a medium where we put all medias on it. Such a medium involves written text(W), images or videos(V) and other electronic information(E). Also, when we actually doing the presentation, we will need to use language(O) or signs(N) to deliver the ideas.
  • Report: W + V
    • A report has written text(W) and images inside(V).
  • Speech: W + O
    • We need to use language(O) or signs(N) to deliver the ideas.

FR Introduction and Conclusions/Recommendations

How do you understand the difference between Conclusions and Recommendations in a feasibility report?

The conclusion made in a feasibility report is a continuity of all the evaluation and research. It is simply a logical comparison process with the objective perspective. A conclusion will be like “after doing some specific research, alternative 1 is good in which way while alternative 2 is good in other perspectives”. Conclusion is more of a summary of all the information that has been gathered for a particular topic. It is a re-statement or re-introduction of everything contained in the report.

The recommendation is then made based on the conclusion to decide which alternative should be taken at the moment or what could be done in the future to make some specific alternatives more viable. A recommendation will be like “the best solution here is alternative 2 because some specific reason”. It is a decision chosen from both logical comparisons and personal judgements.

These two sections has different functionalities: the conclusion part provides information about all solutions while recommendation part selects one of the option and persuade the client to take. In the Chinese philosophy of communication, it provides both depth and breadth on the same topic. The are independent to each other and make the feasibility report more complete.

Blog Posting 6. Feasibility Reports

Part 1: Comment on Chapter 26

Chapter 26 and the idea of feasibility report behind it provide a very good way of talking about alternatives. Problems that that we are trying to solve in real world are mostly open-ended. The ones who demand the problem to be solved cares more about the result rather than the process. More specifically, they will care about the features, cost etc instead of the technology applied. They need a black-box model and we, as problem-solvers, are those who build such a black-box model for them.

Therefore the feasibility report will be our display of alternatives with pros and cons that help the client to make the decision. It gonna be a reflection of our customer research and implementation planning. So we will have a introduction section to show the general problem(it is like a simplified version of introduction in the problem statement) and We will need to find a measureable way to evaluate all those alternatives. That’s why we have a criteria and evaluation section in the report. Also, methods section will show how we gather information for the evaluation process and make it more convincing. At the end of the report, conclusion and recommendation section will give a guideline on how to choose the alternative and possible future steps.

Part 2: Examine the feasibility report samples

Both feasibility reports follow the same superstructure provided in the book. They all have the required 7 sections like what we can see on page 542 of the text book.

Sample 1 did a very good job by following the structure. And in criteria, methods and evaluation section, it uses categorization and bullet point to make it more organized and clear. And the writer also included diagrams and charts to help the evaluation process which is more convincing.

Sample 2 used the similar organization techniques that sample 1 did. Categorization and bullet points make the report concise and precise. And the writer provide us the evaluation chart before the actual details which is more straightforward.
