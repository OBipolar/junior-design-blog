Parallelism

Explain “parallelism” using CS-Related Terms

  • Non-parallel Version

    Streams(Node.js)

    Our responsibilities include:

    • Address stream issues on the issue tracker.
    • Authoring and editing stream documentation within the project.
    • Reviewing changes to stream subclasses within the project.
    • Redirect changes to streams from the project to this project.
    • Implementation assistance of stream providers.
    • Recommending versions of readable-stream to be included.
    • Message communication about the future of streams to give the community advance notice of changes.

    The above list is not parallel because they do not start with the same tense or word, some of them use the form of “-ing”, some use verb and some use nouns.

I will use examples from CS field to illustrate different types of definitions

Stipulation

We usually use Node to refer to the Node.js framework.

Operational Definition

Data in MongoDB has a flexible schema. Collections do not enforce document structure. Decisions that affect how you model data can affect application performance and database capacity. See Data Modeling Concepts for a full high level overview of data modeling in MongoDB.

Antonym

Asynchronous - Synchronous

Examples

As an introduction to the use of MongoDB for operational intelligence and real time analytics use, the document “Storing Log Data” describes several ways and approaches to modeling and storing machine generated data with MongoDB. Then, “Pre-Aggregated Reports” describes methods and strategies for processing data to generate aggregated reports from raw event-data. Finally “Hierarchical Aggregation” presents a method for using MongoDB to process and store hierarchical reports (i.e. per-minute, per-hour, and per-day) from raw event data.

Fomral Definition

An algorithm is a procedure or formula for solving a problem. The word derives from the name of the mathematician, Mohammed ibn-Musa al-Khwarizmi, who was part of the royal court in Baghdad and who lived from about 780 to 850.

Synonym

Computer vision enables images, or sequences of images, to be processed by a computer using an algorithm. The aim of the algorithm is to extract information from the image. Sometimes this information produces an updated image or sometimes other measurements. We can also call this process image processing.

Etymology

BASIC -
computer language, 1964, initialism (acronym) for Beginners’ All-purpose Symbolic Instruction Code; invented by Hungarian-born U.S. computer scientist John G. Kemeny (1926-1992) and U.S. computer scientist Thomas E. Kurtz (b.1928).

Negatives

Typically dynamic. Records can add new information on the fly, and unlike SQL table rows, dissimilar data can be stored together as necessary. For some databases (e.g., wide-column stores), it is somewhat more challenging to add new fields dynamically.

Analogy

Asynchronous - I write you a letter. I put it to the postoffice, and it will be sent to you. I the meantime I do NOT WAIT. I do many different other things. Then you receive the letter. You read it while I still do many different other things. Then you write me an answer and send it to me. In all those things I am not involved. At the next day I get a (synchronous) message (a signal) from the system (postman). It (he) says: “Here is a message for you”. Alternatively I could poll my inbox every five minutes to check if a new letter is there. Then I pause my other work, receive your letter and read your answer. Then I do something according to this answer. But this are things you will not notice, because you are not involved in what I do with your asynchronous answer.

Blog Post 3. Team Work and Working with Clients

Chapter 19

I would like to talk about a negative experience I have when working on a team project. It is impossible to assign tasks evenly to every single one of the team and sometimes this discrepancy will result in more conflicts between group members. There’s a time when I and a couple of friends gather together and work for a Software Engineering class. We split the work into several parts: write ups , UI/UX, front-end, back-end and testing. Each one will be responsible for one part. We separate those work by functions not by work load. It turns out the one who works on the back-end need to spend much more time since he also needs to work on the implementation of database. Then we have a argument since he feels it is unfair for him to work that much. As the team leader, I spent lots of time to help coordinate but both sides were not happy with the collaboration. I think Anderson’s guideline that we need to establish shared responsibility for team progress and cohesion gonna be very useful here. I assign the work based on each one’s expertise and if you take a part you need to be responsible for that part. And sometimes it is not fair to judge based on actual workload. If I can I will make each one’s progress more transparent so that everyone can get know their contribution corresponds to the team’s general progress and I believe it will make the collaboration part more smooth.

Topic Sentence

Presentation Summary

It is very important to have topic Sentence because people will decide whether they will keep reading the article by reading the topic sentence. We should put the topic sentence at the beginning since readers have the intention to skip through the whole article. Topic sentence needs to be concise and precise.

Topic Sentence Analysis

  • The CAT scan creates an image resembling a “slice” that clearly visualizes anatomical structures within the body.
    • The sentence presents a fact about the image that CAT scan can bring us. So the following paragraph will talk about how this image works in details.
  • Improper downstroke and follow-through can cause the golf ball to either hook and fade to the left or slice and fade to the right.
    • The sentence lists two different results of Improper downstroke and follow-through. So the paragraph will talk about these two kinds of results with details description.
  • Three main types of parachutes are used for skydiving. The most widely used parachute has a round, dome-like canopy.
    • The sentence points out 3 main types but only mentions one so far. The paragraph will describe all 3 types of parachutes.
  • A square parachute provides more maneuverability and a better overall ride than does a conventional round parachute.
    • The sentence make a comparison between two different kinds of parachutes but without proofs. The paragraph will explain why one is better than another one with proofs.
  • Two methods of disinfecting treated wastewater are chlorination and ozonation.
    • The sentence list 2 different methods so the paragraph will provide more information on those two methods .
  • The chlorinator room contains the evaporators, chlorinators, and injectors, three of each. The evaporators are used only when liquid chlorine is being drawn from the containers.
    • The sentence talks about three different components in the chlorinator room so the paragraph will talk about each of them in details.

I found the guidelines identify the major kinds of information your reader wants from your communication and describe the way your reader will look for the information very interesting, they correspond to the reader-centered approach that we talked in the class. Chapter 4

According to the reading on chapter 4, the three most helpful guidelines are:

  • Identify your readers’ questions and subquestions
  • avoid including information your readers don’t need
  • Organize hierarchically

The first two guidelines illustrate a very good point similar to the Requirement idea we have during the class. When talking about usefulness, we actually talk about the usefulness for the user which is the reader here. Getting exactly what the want can be considered as the most useful. If we provide information that beyond their need, even if the information might be a brilliant idea. But since it is not related with the need, they may not even care about that. Preciseness and Conciseness are two very important components of usefulness.

Organize hierarchically is also very helpful since it makes sure that we can get access to stuff we want fast and precisely. Sometimes useful information might be hidden in the terrible layout. In order to make you point stand out, it will be a very wise choice to make a good organization.

Chapter 5

According to my reading, I think the three most helpful guidelines from this chapter are:

  • Present sufficient and reliable evidence
  • respond to—and learn from—your readers’ concerns and counterarguments
  • Establish your credibility

Present sufficient and reliable evidence is very helpful since it address on the core of persuasion that you are trying to convince others. Plain theories or ideas will not be enough. Like we will always start our pitch by a background story. Sufficient and reliable evidence will make add more details to the abstract idea and make it easier for the receiver to understand.

Respond to—and learn from—your readers’ concerns and counterarguments is another useful guideline. It uses the same idea of thinking from the perspective of reader. When we switch the role, we can understand what we want better. If the speech right hit what I want then it is definitely a good persuasion.

Establish your credibility talks about long-term persuasiveness. When we are trying to convince people, two things will be very important. One is the idea and another one is yourself. Like those venture capitalists always say, they invest in people rather than ideas. Once you have created your own credibility in a specific area. You can easily come up with a idea with lots of supports.

Blog Posting 1. Reader-Centered Communication and Writing Emails

Chapter 1

The idea that Think Constantly about your readers is very interesting to me. And all the things mentioned in this chapter actually correspond to my experience of summer internship. For the summer internship, I worked as a software developer. One thing I realized most from the internship is that in industry, you will never work alone and you have to cooperate with your co-workers. A large project will usually be split into several parts, and communication here is the key to adhere separate parts together.

One project I did during the summer is a data analysis, which I need to report to both CEO and CTO. Therefore I need to satisfy different readers in a single communication. The CEO wants to understand what does the statistic means and the CTO wants to know how I process the data. Thus I used multiple charts and short text descriptions to show the relationship hidden in the data in favor of the CEO and paste necessary code for the need of CTO. And it turns out to be a good balance. The one wants the analysis result can directly go to the charts and the one wants the processing part can directly go to the code.

I think what I read from this chapter will be very essential in my future work. The Reader-Centered approach is actually very helpful. It can be applied no matter who do you work for. If you work in a big corporation, you definitely needs to write easy-to-read text for administrative people to understand. If you work for a engineer-driven company, you should include technical details as much as possible. It is actually a “fit-in” problem.
